<br> #### Week 3: kinetic sculpture This week the focus was on making a sculpture of some sort that moves using a motor. I chose to make an ode to my car. I intended to make a side profile of a Dodge Challenger with a back wheel spinning and smoke rising behind it. In order to make the profile of the car I imposed an image into Fusion 360 using the canvas tool. ![First Try](./canvastool.png) ![ChallengerinFusion](./challengerinfusion.png) Then I went about outlining the shape of the challenger. ![sketched](./sketched.png) Below is the final result. ![sideprofile](./tada.png) As visible above, the shape of this profile is very curved and this took me a very long time. In the process of sketching this profile, I had to use the spline tool a lot in order to match the curves of the challenger's body. The next step was cutting the profile out. I had been cutting all of my cads incorrectly for a long time, hence this step took a lot longer than expected. I lucked out eventually. However it wasn't until the next week that I learned the right way of printing. This was by far the largest hindrance in my project. As well as the body I also wanted to create an artificial smoke effect using cams. I would stick a dowel on top of the cam and put a piece of paper on it to make it look like smoke. ![cams](./cams.jpg) It was a very simple design that turned out to be very flawed. The first issue I had was material. I was using cardboard and the edge of cardboard is not exactly smooth. This causes issues when trying to move vertically. Also, the vertical line causes rough movements. IF I had used a slanted line, it would have likely functioned smoother. The square hole in the center was fixed out to fit the axel. The axel I was using was a rectangular wooden dowel that was about 3 millimeters by meters on its face. ![axelrose](./axelsize.jpg) The assembly was a very difficult process for me since, as mentioned earlier, I did not cut anything correctly until very recently and I ran out of time in the lab. I was forced to improvise. ![camsattach](./camsattach.jpg) This might be the ugliest project you have ever seen, but it certainly is a project.
